About me

So you want to know a bit more about me...

Well, thank you for your interest!
My name is Jaume Nart Baron and I'm from Barcelona, Spain.
Since I was a little kid, I've been passionate about building stuff, tinkering pretty much everything and understanding how things work under the hood. It was pretty obvious I would end up as an engineer, and so I did.

I was originally supposed to become an engineer but the thought of having to expend my creative energy on things that make practical everyday life even more refined, with a loathsome capital gain as the goal, was unbearable to me.

Albert Einstein

Isn't he right tough? Well, this blog aims to be the main drain of my creative energy technology-wise. Here I will post all my hobbyist technology-related projects, explained step-by-step so anyone can replicate them and learn.

Topics I'm passionate about:

Mechatronics & rapid prototyping

Building a DIY 3D printer from scratch when I was 21 was by far the most important milestone for me as an engineer. It's funny how somehting I did just for curiosity in my free time, turned out to be the beginning of my passion for building, developing and prototyping.


Internet of things

My passion for electronics, networking, data analytics and data visualization in a single application? That's the internet of things. There are plenty of open-source technologies that give hobbyists and engineers limitless possibilities. For instance, I've already built my own home automation system which among other things, lets me know if someone entered my room while I'm not there.


Artificial Intelligence

Machines that learn, make decisions and can find patterns in data that humans are uncapable of. That's what artificial intelligence is all about. These technologies will disrupt everything as we know it today during the coming years. Object detection, language processing and machine learning are some of the fields I'm more interested about.

How am I going to do all this?

Python is one of the most powerful and versatile languages out there today. Machine Learning, Image Processing, Web Development, Data Analytics, Time Travel. You can do anything with it. Besides, python is very easy to learn and understand for its syntax. I also might be using some other languages like Javascript, Java, C++, HTML, CSS, etc...

About this blog

The Self-Engineer

What is that? What is a self-engineer? It is basically the DIY approach to learning, building and getting things done.
This name came up to me one day I realized that most of the knowledge I acquire comes from the self-study, being the internet and the community living there the main source.
Education is changing, the knowledge shifted from schools and universities to a public source available to anyone called internet. There are tons of people sharing knowledge, enabling others to learn and making the internet the new land of opportunities. That I want to do with this blog is to contribute to this community. In the future, a computer will be the greatest expression of freedom.
