About me

So you want to know a bit more about me...

Well, thanks for the interest! My name is Jaume Nart Baron and I'm from Barcelona, Spain. Since I was a little kid, I've been passionate about building stuff, tinkering pretty much everything and understanding how things work under the hood. It was pretty obvious I would end up as an engineer, and so I did.

I was originally supposed to become an engineer but the thought of having to expend my creative energy on things that make practical everyday life even more refined, with a loathsome capital gain as the goal, was unbearable to me.

Albert Einstein

Isn't he right tough? Well, this blog aims to be the main drain of my creative energy. Here I will post all my technology-related projects, explained step-by-step so anyone can replicate them and learn. My main interests are Internet of Things, Artificial Intelligence and Mechatronics.

I like to do other things though

Travel the world

Get to know other cultures, speak other languages, meet new people are things are deeply enjoy. I've had the opportunity to live in Italy, France, Poland and Belgium and the list will probably get larger. I speak fluently 5 languages and I have friends from all over the world.


Train the mind and the body

I've always been a sports person. I've been playing basketball since I was 10 years old. I can't number all the values and principles that this fantastic sport has taught me. Wherever I may travel, basketball will follow.


Feel the music

Find three hobbies you love: One to make you money, one to keep you in shape, and one to be creative. Me and my guitar, we are an inseparable pack. I also sing but just for me, because I'm actually fairly bad.

About this blog

The Self-Engineer

What is that? What is a self-engineer? It is basically the DIY approach to learning, building and getting things done.
This name came up to me one day I realized that most of the knowledge I acquire comes from the self-study, being the internet and the community living there the main source.
Education is changing, the knowledge shifted from schools and universities to a public source available to anyone called internet. There are tons of people sharing knowledge, enabling others to learn and making the internet the new land of opportunities. That I want to do with this blog is to contribute to this community. In the future, a computer will be the greatest expression of freedom.
